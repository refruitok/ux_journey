The User Journey Generator is a decision-support environment for UX, product, and service teams to model, branch, and evaluate multiple plausible user journeys under uncertainty — before commitment, build, or rollout.

Rather than producing a single “happy path,” the system enables teams to generate, compare, and stress-test alternative journeys, making trade-offs, risks, and missed opportunities visible early.

Purpose

The generator is designed to support three critical activities:

Faster synthesis across research signals, hypotheses, and design intent
Systematic option exploration, instead of premature convergence
Early exposure of assumptions, before they solidify into decisions

By exploring multiple paths in parallel, teams can reason about why different outcomes emerge — not just what happens.

Frameworks in use

Google Micro-Moments

Models intent-driven decision thresholds (Know, Go, Do, Buy) as branching points rather than fixed stages.

Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)

Grounds each journey step in functional, emotional, and situational jobs, clarifying motivations behind divergence.

Sludge & Friction Audit

Detects hesitation, cognitive load, ambiguity, and trust gaps that cause users to stall, detour, or abandon.

What the system produces

For each persona–objective pair, the simulator produces a baseline journey plus multiple alternative paths, with explicit representation of touchpoints, emotional states, and hesitation factors.

Quantitative scoring for CX quality and time/effort cost.
Intervention opportunities identified per step and per path.
Structured outputs ready for export and review.
"Most journey work fails not because teams choose the wrong solution, but because they never explored the full option space."

Why this matters

This generator is designed to delay false certainty by making alternative futures explicit, comparable, and discussable. It helps teams avoid common failure modes like locking onto a single path too early or treating edge cases as noise.

Intended use

  • Early-stage journey exploration
  • Pre-implementation readiness assessment
  • Scenario planning and risk evaluation
  • Stakeholder alignment under constraints
  • Design and product decision audits

Bring Your Own Key (BYOK)

This application operates on a BYOK model. Your data and keys are stored locally in your browser and are never sent to our servers. You will need your own API keys to power the intelligence and visual generation.
